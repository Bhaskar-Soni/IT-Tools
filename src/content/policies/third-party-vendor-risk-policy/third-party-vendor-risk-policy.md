# Third-Party Vendor Risk Policy

## 1. Purpose
Define vendor risk controls for external services, tools, and dependencies that impact repository security, privacy, or availability.

## 2. Scope
Includes SaaS platforms, cloud services, code scanning vendors, dependency registries, and service providers with direct or indirect data access.

## 3. Vendor Risk Lifecycle
1. Intake and business justification.
2. Due diligence and risk tiering.
3. Contractual control validation.
4. Ongoing monitoring.
5. Offboarding and access revocation.

## 4. Vendor Tiering
Tier | Criteria | Minimum Requirements
--- | --- | ---
High | Access to sensitive data or privileged operations | Security review, contract controls, annual reassessment
Medium | Limited operational dependency | Security checklist, periodic review
Low | Minimal impact/no sensitive access | Lightweight evaluation

## 5. Minimum Due Diligence
* Security posture questionnaire.
* Data handling and retention validation.
* Incident notification commitments.
* Access and authentication controls.
* Business continuity and backup capability.

## 6. Contractual Requirements
* Confidentiality and data use limitations.
* Breach notification timelines.
* Right-to-audit or assurance reporting access.
* Defined offboarding and data deletion commitments.

## 7. Continuous Monitoring
* Review vendor control attestations where available.
* Reassess high-risk vendors annually.
* Trigger reassessment after material incidents or platform changes.

## 8. Offboarding Requirements
* Revoke integrations and tokens immediately when no longer needed.
* Confirm data return/deletion process.
* Archive required evidence of offboarding completion.
