# Regulatory Compliance Management Policy

## 1. Purpose
Establish how compliance obligations are identified, implemented, monitored, and evidenced for repository operations and related systems.

## 2. Scope
Covers contractual, legal, and internal policy obligations relevant to source control, security, privacy, and operational integrity.

## 3. Compliance Program Requirements
* Maintain a current compliance obligations inventory.
* Map obligations to technical and procedural controls.
* Define accountable owners for each obligation.
* Track implementation status and open gaps.

## 4. Control Mapping and Validation
1. Identify each obligation and applicable systems.
2. Map to existing controls and ownership.
3. Validate effectiveness through periodic checks.
4. Record evidence and remediation actions.

## 5. Evidence Management
Evidence Type | Examples | Retention
--- | --- | ---
Control evidence | Access review results, scanner outputs | 12 months minimum
Operational evidence | Incident records, remediation tickets | 24 months minimum
Approval evidence | Exception approvals, change sign-offs | 12 months minimum

## 6. Assessment Frequency
* Quarterly control self-assessments.
* Annual formal compliance review.
* Ad-hoc reassessment after major architecture/process change.

## 7. Non-Compliance Handling
* Log non-conformance with severity and business impact.
* Assign corrective action owner and due date.
* Escalate overdue high-severity findings to governance owners.

## 8. Exception Management
* Exceptions require documented justification and compensating controls.
* Exceptions must include expiry and periodic review cadence.
* Expired exceptions without renewal are invalid.

## 9. Reporting
* Monthly compliance dashboard to policy owner and maintainers.
* Immediate escalation for critical control failures.
