# Privacy By Design Policy

## 1. Purpose
Ensure privacy requirements are integrated into design, development, and operation of repository-related features and workflows.

## 2. Scope
Applies to any processing of personal data in code, logs, analytics, documentation, support processes, and integrations.

## 3. Privacy by Design Principles
* Proactive not reactive.
* Privacy as the default setting.
* Data minimization and purpose limitation.
* End-to-end data security.
* Transparency and accountability.

## 4. Mandatory Design Requirements
* Define purpose and legal basis before collecting personal data.
* Collect only required attributes.
* Set default retention and deletion behavior.
* Avoid using personal data in non-production unless anonymized.

## 5. Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) Triggers
Perform DPIA when:
* Processing involves sensitive categories at scale.
* New high-risk profiling/monitoring functionality is introduced.
* Cross-border or third-party data sharing materially changes.

## 6. User Rights Support
Systems and processes must support:
* Access requests.
* Rectification.
* Deletion where legally permitted.
* Processing restriction/objection where applicable.

## 7. Privacy in Logging and Telemetry
* Do not log unnecessary personal identifiers.
* Mask/pseudonymize where feasible.
* Restrict access to logs with personal data elements.

## 8. Governance and Review
* Privacy controls must be reviewed during architecture and PR reviews for relevant changes.
* High-risk changes require explicit privacy sign-off.
